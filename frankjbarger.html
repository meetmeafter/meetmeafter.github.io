MEET FRANK BARGER

Born in 1937 in Altoona, Pennsylvania, Frank served his country in Vietnam before setting up a career at the Pennsylvania Railroad. Frank died in 2017. This is Frank's story - told from his perspective.

"I can't think of any other way I would have wanted to live my life. It pretty much went as good as it could've. I'm a blessed man."
-Frank Barger

BORN: February 21, 1937

DIED: January 16, 2017 (Age 79)

OBITUARY: Frank Barger, 79, Altoona, passed away Sunday at UPMC Altoona following an extended illness. He was born in Altoona, son of the late Rita and Louis Barger. On March 12, 1959, he married Dorothy who survives him as well as two sons, Jack and Randy, both of Altoona, and a daughter, Amy, of Harrisburg. Frank served his country with pride in the Vietnam War, where he received a Bronze Star for valor. He retired as a repairman for the Pennsylvania Railroad in 1989 after 30 years of service. Frank enjoyed spending time with his grandchildren, Dominic and Sheila, who will miss their "PapPap." Frank spent his free time hunting, playing cards with his Wednesday night crew, and his daily trips to the Farmers Market. In lieu of flowers, donation may be made to Saint Mark's Catholic Church, Altoona, where Frank was a lifelong parishoner.

Frank's favorite poem:

by Mary Elizabeth Frye

Do not stand at my grave and weep,

I am not there, I do not sleep.

I am in a thousand winds that blow,

I am the softly falling snow.

I am the gentle showers of rain,

I am the fields of ripening grain.

I am in the morning hush,

I am in the graceful rush

Of beautiful birds in circling flight,

I am the starshine of the night.

I am in the flowers that bloom,

I am in a quiet room.

I am in the birds that sing,

I am in each lovely thing.

Do not stand at my grave and cry,

I am not there. I do not die.
